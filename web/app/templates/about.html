Summary

The objective of this fellowship is to set up a rooftop personal weather station on the CUNY Advanced Science Research Center building. The intent of this project is to collect local weather data and compare it to data-sets from other stations across New York City such as those at Central Park, JFK, and LaGuardia. The unit being used is Davis Vantage Pro2+ personal weather station. The information collected from this weather station will provide useful data such as frequent updates of rainfall quantities, barometric air pressure, wind direction, wind speed, UV radiation and solar radiation, and forecasted weather for the next 12 hours. This data will be compared with data from other weather stations across New York City to aid in the analysis of urban heat island locations, flooding and drainage requirements due to precipitation, and to visualize weather patterns across Metropolitan area of New York City. The data from the Vantage Pro2 will be primarily be used to facilitate student research at the ASRC. The collected data also has potential to be used by other scientists and engineers for industry related use as it will be uploaded to platforms such as WeatherLink and Weather Underground where it is accessible to the public. This weather station is a prototype for a larger network of personal weather stations which will be deployed throughout other locations in New York City. After initial setup, the Vantage Pro2 will need to be maintained regularly through once a week inspections of hardware failure due to exposure to the elements. This includes maintenance of the anemometer, rain collector cups, and ensuring the unit is clear of debris and grime. Weekly checks to ensure that data is continuously collected and stored securely. These steps will facilitate future research which has potential be used for projects which can aid in city planning to accommodate the everchanging weather and climate.
